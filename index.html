Introduction

Overview

Background

  • 64% of Americans own a smartphone [3]
  • 90% of Americans own a cell phone [3]
  • 74% of smartphone users allow 'current location' access [3]
  • 396 Million Facebook users only use the service on a phone [4]

Background

Standard Mobile Device Features:

  • Microphone (Developer Access)
  • Multiple Cameras (Developer Access)
  • Location Sensor (Developer Access)
  • Fingerprint Scanner (NEW:Developer Access)
  • Payment Systems (Developer Access)
  • Owner Information

Ethical Issues

Who can access the information on our devices:

  • The user
  • The government
  • Network providers
  • Developers
  • Malicious users

The Government

    Patriot Act: Title II

  • "In general, the Title expands federal agencies' powers in intercepting, sharing, and using private telecommunications, especially electronic communications..." [8]

    • City of Seattle: StingRay

  • "Certainly, we (and the general public too) understand that law enforcement has the ability to "ping" a cell phone for its location..." [6]

Developers

    Marauders Map

  • “I decided to write this extension, because we are constantly being told how we are losing privacy with the increasing digitization of our lives, however the consequences never seem tangible. With this code you can see for yourself the potentially invasive usage of the information you share, and decide for yourself if this is something you should worry about,” [9]

    • Terms of Service

  • "..there's no way the average person could keep up with all the data-sucking caveats buried in the privacy policies of the sites and services they use." [10]

Conclusions

  • What data are you sharing?
  • What access should the government be allowed?
  • What access should the developers be allowed?
  • Who is responsible for your privacy?

Recommendations

  1. Focus on what you can control:
    • Protect your own privacy above all else
    • Pay attention to Privacy Policies, Terms of Service, etc.
    • Be aware of what data applications have access to
  2. Stand up for privacy:
    • The Patriot Act
    • The Trans-Pacific Partnership (TPP)
    • Local government: Stingray use
    • Build/engineer with privacy as a concern
